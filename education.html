Education

All of my undergraduate courses were taken between Fall 2015 and Spring 2019 at Saint Louis University.

Biomedical Engineering Primary Major

Signals and Systems (BME3100)

Piqued my interest in medical devices and biomedical mathematics. Included topics in differential equations, ciruit design, and MATLAB development


Biomedical Instrumentation (BME3150)

CURRENTLY ENROLLED

Overview of devices used in medical processes. Included introduction to imaging, brain-computer interface, neurological and physiological pathways, and other topics.


Mechanics (BME3200)

Problem solving for static and dynamic physics. A focus on basic physics concepts including force, torque, mechanical failure, and stability.


Transport Fundamentals (BME 3300)

Introduction to fluid dynamics as an expansion of thermodynamics. This course included concepts directly related to medical transport, including drug delivery, the cardiovascular system, and flow relationships between tissue layers.


Materials Science (BME 3400)

Extension of chemistry with a focus on structure of different solid types, including polymers, metals, ceramics, and composites.


Junior Lab (BME3840)

Great team leadership and collaboration experience. Most experiment procedures were unorganized, missing critical components, and complete failures, but the learning experience was focused on team building, which made the course a good experience.


Medical Imaging (BME4130)

CURRENTLY ENROLLED

Concepts in medical physics relating to analysis of photon based signals. Discussion included engineering of medical imaging devices such as X-Ray, Magnetic Resonance, Computed Tomography, Ultrasound, and others.

Computer Science Secondary Major

Data Structures (CSCI 2100)

Introduced computer science fundamentals including stacks, heaps, queues, deques, and many other data structures. Incorporated discrete concepts including Big O notation relating to the nature of binary systems.


Object Oriented Software Design (CSCI 2300)

Took as independent study. This course introduced high level software design through the use of Java Swing libraries, team projects, and UML planning.


Computer Architecture (CSCI 2400)

This course was an introduction to low level software design, hardware-software interfacing, and programming in C. I enjoyed this course a lot, and plan to continue using the knowledge I gained in this course to develop new medical technologies and devices.


Unix Tools (CSCI 2930)

CURRENTLY ENROLLED

Basic concepts and techniques for running scripts on Unix based operating systems.


Programming Languages (CSCI 3200)

CURRENTLY ENROLLED

Discussion of compilers and the essential characteristics of programming languages. This course helps to broaden understanding of turing-complete languages and gain insights on languages based on their characteristics rather than syntax.


Software Engineering (CSCI 3300)

CURRENTLY ENROLLED

Agile development and business structure concepts critical to industry programmers and software developers. This course incorporated a group project discussed in Research


Operating Systems (CSCI 3500)

Another excellent course in low-level computer science. Transactions between different software layers was discussed as an extension of what was learned in Computer Architecture


Artificial Intelligence (CSCI 3760)

An introduction to heuristic learning, tensor flow, machine learning, and other aspects of computer intelligence. I took this course because I am very interested in the relationship between computer learning and human learning.


Machine Learning (CSCI 4930)

CURRENTLY ENROLLED

A course that expands on topics of artificial intelligence, networks, flow, and critical aspects to reinforced and non-reinforced learning.


Capstone Project I/II (CSCI 4961/4962)

CURRENTLY ENROLLED

Senior Project based on development of a user interface for concept maps of biological processes. This project is based in MEAN Stack website development following the Agile software engineering process. The critical components of this project include feedback-response agents, user log in utilizing Google's OAuth 2.0 (SAML) authentication interface, and a complete understanding of JavaScript website integration.

Engineering Mathematics Minor

Discrete Mathematics (MATH1660)

Discrete functions, Big O, modular arithmetic, encryption, and various other concepts relating to discrete operations. This was a fun course that allowed me to expand my understanding of mathematics in coding projects


Calculus II (MATH1520)

Interesting concepts involving differentiation, integration, polynomial function expansion using taylor series, and function convergence.


Calculus III (MATH2530)

Introduction to multivariable calculus, including three dimensional spatial understanding, gradient classification, simple linear algebra, and others.


Linear Algebra For Engineers (MATH3110)

Excellent course that discussed various advanced techinques for solving systems of equations, matrix properties, decompositions and their applications, etc. This was my favorite course taken in university.


Differential Equations I (MATH3550)

Discussion of differential calculus, involving multivariable arithmetic, generalization for first and second order differential equation solutions, and some chaos theory. Fun problem solving!


Foundations of Statistics (MATH3850)

Statistical analysis and problem solving utilizing the programming language R. Very general concepts in distribution classification, simulation, and data analysis were discussed.
